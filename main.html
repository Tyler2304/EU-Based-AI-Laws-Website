EU based AI Laws and Legislation

What Exactly is the EU AI Act?

The EU AI Act is legislation provided by the EU to help regulate and keep AI systems following the specific guidelines. This is a legal framework aimed at protecting the safety and rights of individual users.

How does the EU AI Act affect me?

If you are located within the EU, this framework and legislation apply to you when dealing with any kind of AI. If you were to deploy a form of AI in a professional manner, you are obligated to follow these guidelines. Companies based out of the EU still have the act applied to them if their AI is used within the EU. As well as this, there are some exceptions for this framework. If the AI is used exclusively for military and defense purposes, they are exempt.

How does the Act improve Cybersecurity?

Within this act, it specifically states that developers of high risk systems are legally required to have security against data poisoning and model poisoning. This is to ensure that systems are appropriately defended against these high threat AI based cyber attacks. The entire lifecycle of these AI systems must be monitored and kept secure the entire time. Finally, a high risk system should have a kill switch implemented so that any human user can shut the system down.

What other AI regulations are there?

Within the EU, there is no other AI regulations to cover specifically AI systems currently. The only legislations that protects users from AI-related risks are the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Network and Information Security (NIS) Directive.

How does AI impact cyberattacks?

Cyber attacks alone are a massive threat to online systems but when AI is implemented into these already existing threats it becomes a lot more dangerous. As a standard for these attacks, AI is able to make them more frequent and harder to detect. Social engineering attacks are adapted to become more realistic and increase the likelihood of the attack working. Automation is a big part of AI based cyber attacks, the malicious users are able to automate the attacks and have them happen at a frequent and regular rate.

Can AI be used for good purposes?

Despite all the negative aspects of what AI can do when utilised by a malicious user, there are a selection of purposes that are beneficial for society. It can enhance the productivity of users when used correctly. In relation to cybersecurity, AI can be used for defence systems to help protect against any form of cyber attack. Real Time threat detection is a usage of AI based algorithms to monitor traffic to help block any form of threat immediately.

Within the EU, what are the legal issues with failing to secure an AI system?

There can be severe legal consequences when your AI system does not follow the correct frameworks such as the EU AI Act. The framework exists to protect and keep users safe so when developers fail to meet these requirements it can cause issues for systems. These regulations mandate that anyone not following this legislation may receive massive financial penalties and civil liability. This is an incentive for those developers to keep their systems regulated. It may also help users to feel more safe when these regulations exist.

Why is it important for you to understand the AI Laws?

In order to ensure your safety and legal compliance online, understanding what laws exist is important. Within the EU AI Act, they section the laws based on the risk levels: minimal, limited, high and unacceptable risks. These laws exist to prevent users from creating illegal and dangerous content utilising AI. A transparency regulation requires that AI generated content be explicitly labelled as such to protect users. Using AI without following the legal guidelines will lead to potential fines and legal issues.

How do cybercriminals use AI?

Cybercriminals utilise AI to attack online users. Some of these attacks are AI enhanced phishing attacks, automated vulnerability discovery, deepfake attacks and AI driven ransomware. These are a handful of the dangerous and scary attacks that criminals are able to use to attack unsuspecting victims. The range and potency of the threats may alter based on what data is at risk however, it is all illegal and a threat nonetheless.

What is predictive cybersecurity?

There is a defence method that can be used to help protect and prevent AI based cyberattacks from ever occurring. It identifies potential vulnerabilities and the behaviour of attackers to predict the attack before it begins. This technology will enable systems to learn user patterns and therefore be able to flag possible risks. By utilising this they are able to predict the next possible attacks and defend accordingly. For example, an employee accessing files they don't usually may raise a risk in the system. This will be effective against zero day exploits that may have not been used or seen before.

What is Cyber Hygiene?

A cyber routine is a positive practice to have that ensures individuals remain as safe as they can be online. It consists of protecting data and improving digital security by means of consistent habits. Updating software, using strong complex passwords or enabling multi factor authentication. Getting into a routine of ensuring you are as safe as possible will help to prevent any attack in the first place. It keeps your data and devices secure.

What is Data Poisoning?

A special form of attack that operates against AI systems is data poisoning. This is a form of a backdoor attack where attackers poison the training data that models use. A certain phrase or trigger will set this malicious training data off and then can make these models potentially release sensitive and harmful data. For example, an attacker may infect the content on websites or forums that have public access and models will use to train their data sets on.
